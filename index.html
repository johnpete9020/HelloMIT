From Your Notes (core facts)

  • Power: \\((x^n)'=n x^{n-1}\\).
  • Exponential: \\((e^{rx})'=r e^{rx}\\), \\((a^x)'=a^x\\ln a\\).
  • Trig: \\((\\sin x)'=\\cos x\\), \\((\\cos x)'=-\\sin x\\), \\((\\tan x)'=\\sec^2 x\\).
  • Inverse trig: \\((\\tan^{-1}x)'=\\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}\\).
  • Log: \\((\\ln x)'=\\dfrac{1}{x}\\), and \\((\\ln(ax))'=\\dfrac{1}{x}\\) for \\(a>0\\).
  • Linear combos: \\((f+g)'=f'+g'\\), \\((c f)'=c f'\\).
  • Product: \\((fg)'=f'g+fg'\\).
  • Quotient: \\(\\left(\\dfrac{f}{g}\\right)'=\\dfrac{f'g-fg'}{g^2}\\).
  • Chain: \\((f\\circ g)'=f'(g(x))\\,g'(x)\\).

Rules — English & Mathematical

Power Rule

Math: \\(\\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^n)=n x^{n-1}\\); for \\([g(x)]^n\\): \\(n[g(x)]^{n-1}g'(x)\\).

English: If you see \\((\\text{stuff})^n\\), differentiate the outer power and multiply by the derivative of the inside.

Chain Rule

Math: If \\(y=f(g(x))\\), then \\(y'=f'(g(x))g'(x)\\).

English: For nested functions: outer′(inner) × inner′. Peel layers one by one.

Product Rule

Math: \\((fg)'=f'g+fg'\\).

English: Tag the two factors. Differentiate one at a time, then add the terms.

Quotient Rule

Math: \\(\\left(\\dfrac{f}{g}\\right)'=\\dfrac{f'g-fg'}{g^2}\\).

English: “low d‑high − high d‑low, over low‑low.” Square the entire denominator.

Tangent Line

Math: At \\(x=a\\), \\(y-f(a)=f'(a)(x-a)\\).

English: Compute \\(f(a)\\) and slope \\(f'(a)\\). Use point–slope through \\((a,f(a))\\).
